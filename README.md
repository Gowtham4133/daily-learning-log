  >1. <ins>**Statistical knowledge**</ins> is the understanding of concepts and methods for collecting, organizing, analyzing, and interpreting
  >2. <ins>**Data interpretation**</ins> is the process of analyzing and making sense of data, often from visual formats like graphs and tables, to draw conclusions and make informed decisions
  >3. <ins>**Technical documentation**</ins> is any written information that describes the use, purpose, creation, or architecture of a product, system, or service
  >4. <ins>**Hardware troubleshooting**</ins> is the process of diagnosing and fixing problems with a computer's physical components by following a systematic approach that includes checking connections, running diagnostic tools, and testing components
  >    1. Check connections: Ensure all cables and components are securely plugged in. If a device has a power switch or batteries, confirm they are on and charged.
  >    2. Restart the device: A simple restart can often resolve temporary glitches.
  >    3. Use a different port: If a peripheral isn't working, try a different USB port to rule out a faulty port.
  >    4. Perform a visual inspection: Look for any signs of physical damage, such as burnt components or bulging capacitors on the motherboard.
